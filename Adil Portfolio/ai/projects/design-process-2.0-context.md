# DESIGN PROCESS 2.0 — FULL CONTEXT

## Created by Adil Ahmad

## Qatar Airways | Product Design Management

### Transforming how design operates at scale

---

## Overview

When I joined Qatar Airways, one thing became clear very quickly.

The quality of the designers was not the problem.

The process was.

Across multiple teams I was hearing the same frustrations.

Product owners felt design took too long.

Designers felt requirements were unclear.

Stakeholders were joining too late.

Reviews were inconsistent.

Expectations varied depending on the team.

Everyone was working hard, but not always working in the same direction.

The result was unnecessary rework, missed expectations and frustration on both sides.

Instead of treating these as isolated issues, I stepped back and looked at the design organisation as a whole.

The challenge wasn't individual performance.

The challenge was the system.

---

## The Opportunity

I wanted to create a process that would:

- Increase clarity and ownership
- Reduce unnecessary rework
- Improve stakeholder alignment
- Create consistency across teams
- Give designers confidence in what "good" looks like
- Strengthen collaboration between Product, Design and Engineering

Most importantly, I wanted a process that people would actually use.

---

## Research & Discovery

I gathered feedback from designers, product owners and stakeholders across the organisation.

Patterns quickly emerged.

Common themes included:

- Unclear requirements
- Late stakeholder feedback
- Limited visibility of work
- Inconsistent review standards
- Lack of ownership between teams
- Different ways of working across domains

Using these insights, I mapped the existing process and identified the biggest friction points affecting delivery.

---

## Creating Design 2.0

The outcome was Design 2.0.

A redesigned framework that introduced clearer stages, stronger governance and greater visibility throughout the design lifecycle.

The process focused on:

- Structured discovery
- Defined review checkpoints
- Clear stakeholder involvement
- Improved handovers
- Consistent quality standards
- Greater accountability across teams

Rather than adding more process, the goal was to remove ambiguity.

### Process stages (framework detail)

The operating model evolved from a four-step flow (Ideation → Design → QA → Launch) to Design 2.0 with five stages:

**Sprint 0 → Design → QA → Launch → Review**

- **Sprint 0:** Requirements and discovery — alignment and scope clarity before design starts
- **Design:** UI design and UX review — accelerated through components and shared assets
- **QA:** QA review and handover session — visibility and fewer collaboration bottlenecks
- **Launch:** Production and post-launch review — stronger collaboration with engineering
- **Review:** In-review and v2 refinement — learning feeds the next cycle

Supporting culture initiatives included workshops, design and accessibility ramp-up, deep dives and show-and-tell, agency collaboration, a monthly newsletter, and product alignment plus speed reviews.

---

## Impact

Design 2.0 became the standard design process used across Qatar Airways Digital.

Today it supports designers, product owners and stakeholders across multiple teams and initiatives.

The framework has improved alignment, increased consistency and created a shared understanding of how design work moves from idea to delivery.

Beyond improving delivery, it also strengthened my leadership capabilities by allowing me to drive organisational change, influence cross-functional teams and create a framework that continues to scale beyond my direct involvement.

---

## Reflection

One of the biggest lessons from this initiative was that great design outcomes rarely come from talent alone.

They come from creating the right environment for talented people to succeed.

Design 2.0 was never about adding more process.

It was about giving teams the clarity, confidence and structure needed to do their best work.
