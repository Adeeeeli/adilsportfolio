# DESIGN LAB - FULL CONTEXT
## Created by Adil Ahmad
## Qatar Airways | Product Design Management | 2025

### Overview

Design Lab was an internal strategic initiative proposed and created by Adil Ahmad at Qatar Airways to transform how product design operated across the mobile app ecosystem.

The initiative was designed to address fragmentation across digital product pods and introduce a more collaborative, future-focused model for shaping customer experiences.

Rather than designers operating independently within separate pods, Design Lab proposed a self-governed squad model that aligned multiple designers under a shared vision and strategic direction.

The objective was to elevate design from feature execution into a strategic capability responsible for shaping the future customer experience.

---

## The Core Problem

The problem Design Lab aimed to solve was not poor design quality.

The real issue was alignment.

At scale, product teams were operating in pods with designers embedded individually into domains.

This created challenges:

- Designers worked independently with limited cross-domain collaboration.
- Experiences became disconnected across the app ecosystem.
- Teams optimized for roadmap delivery rather than long-term customer experience.
- Innovation became reactive rather than proactive.
- Product thinking happened inside silos.

Although features were being delivered, there was limited opportunity to shape a unified North Star vision for the future mobile experience.

Adil identified this as an organizational design challenge rather than simply a UX problem.

He believed the system of working needed redesigning.

---

## The Vision

Design Lab proposed a different operating model.

Instead of a traditional pod structure where one designer is linked to one squad, Design Lab would function as a dedicated innovation and execution division within design.

The vision was:

"A strategically formed, self-governed design division focused on shaping and delivering extraordinary experiences for today and tomorrow."

The Design Lab team would consist of approximately 4–5 designers operating as one squad.

The team would focus on:

- Future product vision
- Connected experience design
- Cross-domain collaboration
- Design system improvements
- Roadmap influence
- Strategic innovation
- Feature acceleration

The mission was not to replace product pods.

The mission was to support and elevate them.

---

## Why Design Lab Was Needed

Design Lab was created to solve several business and experience challenges.

### 1. Creating Extraordinary Experiences
Product teams often focused on immediate delivery.

Design Lab created space to think beyond incremental improvements and design bigger customer moments.

### 2. Co-defining the Product Roadmap
Rather than design reacting to requirements, Design Lab would help influence future opportunities and strategic direction.

### 3. Strengthening the Design System
A shared squad enabled greater consistency across components, patterns and experiences.

### 4. Unlocking Business Opportunities
Design Lab would identify new opportunities for growth and feature innovation.

An example discussed internally was wallet/payment functionality.

### 5. Accelerating Delivery
Instead of relying on isolated design ownership, multiple designers could collaborate to move faster and improve quality.

The team was intentionally designed as a multi-disciplinary squad with complementary strengths rather than isolated contributors.

## The Four-Phase Model

Design Lab followed a phased approach.

### Phase 0: Onboarding

Before implementation, Adil proposed onboarding all stakeholders.

Purpose:
- Educate product owners
- Align PMs and designers
- Introduce the vision
- Establish ways of working

This included workshops and kickoff sessions.

---

### Phase 1: Research & Discovery

Goal:
Understand the full mobile ecosystem.

Activities:
- Senior stakeholder interviews
- Alignment workshops
- Product owner collaboration
- Competitive benchmarking
- Industry analysis
- Customer pain point mapping
- Data-informed design research
- Quantitative and qualitative insights

Deliverable:
A large FigJam containing:
- Current journeys
- Opportunities
- Pain points
- Strategic ideas
- Experience mapping

Outcome:
Full understanding of every domain and future opportunities.

Estimated timeline:
1 month.

---

### Phase 2: Shaping the Vision

Goal:
Design the future North Star experience.

Activities:
- Redesign information architecture
- Reimagine user flows
- Create new UI paradigms
- Challenge industry norms
- Conduct rapid usability testing
- Bi-weekly stakeholder updates
- Monthly leadership reviews
- Iterate based on feedback

Deliverable:
A high-fidelity interactive prototype of the future Qatar Airways app.

Outcome:
A complete end-to-end future-state experience.

Timeline:
Approximately 2 months.

---

### Phase 3: Executive Alignment & Approvals

Goal:
Gain organizational buy-in.

Stakeholders:
- Senior leadership
- Product leadership
- Brand teams
- Executives
- C-level sponsors

Purpose:
- Align all mobile pods
- Secure approval
- Create a shared future direction
- Communicate the North Star vision

Key belief:
"Big visions require belief."

---

### Phase 4: Implementation

Goal:
Deliver the future vision gradually.

Approach:
Rather than redesigning everything at once, Design Lab proposed working backwards.

The North Star would be broken into achievable roadmap increments.

Pods would continue shipping while gradually moving toward the bigger vision.

This allowed:

- Innovation without disruption
- Roadmap continuity
- Progressive transformation

Existing pod designers would eventually be absorbed into Design Lab around later phases.
